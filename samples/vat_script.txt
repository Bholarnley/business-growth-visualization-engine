“Many business owners are still misunderstanding VAT.”
“VAT is not your money.
Your client pays it — you are only collecting it on behalf of the government.
For example, if your service is ₦100,000, your client should be charged ₦107,500.
That ₦7,500 is VAT and must be separated immediately and remitted on or before the 21st of the following month.
Now with Rev360 and the new compliance structure, VAT filing is no longer manual or loose  it is data-driven, structured, and highly traceable.
And if you miss filing deadlines, penalties apply — starting from about ₦50,000, plus additional charges and interest depending on delay.”
“But the real risk is not just penalties.
It’s how VAT is assessed.”
“Not all inflows are VATable — loans are not sales, transfers are not sales, and some NGOs, diplomatic bodies, exports, and VAT-exempt transactions are not taxable.”
“But if your records are not properly classified, Rev360 can apply BOJ — Best of Judgment — and calculate VAT on total inflows at 7.5%.
Meaning you could be taxed on money that was never actual sales.”
“That’s why proper bookkeeping is now critical.
You need a bookkeeper or accountant using systems like QuickBooks, Sage, Odoo, Zoho Books, or ERP tools, depending on business size, to correctly separate VATable and non-VATable transactions before filing.
Because compliance is no longer guesswork  it is structured reporting.”
